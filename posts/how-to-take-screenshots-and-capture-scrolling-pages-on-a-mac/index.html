You can also take a screenshot of a select area of your screen by pressing Command+Shift+4 and then dragging the cross hair to select the area. 2. How to Capture a Scrolling Page Screenshot On Mac#

Capturing a screenshot of a scrolling page on Mac is easier than you may think. Start by opening the page you want to take a screenshot of, then press Command + Shift + 4. If you press and hold the spacebar while doing this, your cursor will become a camera and you can click the page to take the screenshot. The image will be saved as a .png file on your desktop.

Another method for capturing a screenshot of a scrolling page on Mac is to use the Preview app. Start by opening the page and then open the Preview app. Go to the ‘File’ menu and select ‘Take Screenshot from’ and then pick the option that says ‘Selection, Window or Entire Screen’. Once you have selected the option, click and drag around the page you want to capture and the image will be saved.

You can also capture a scrolling page screenshot on Mac using the browser you’re using. For example, if you’re using Safari, click the File menu and select ‘Take Screenshot of’ followed by ‘Selection, Window, or Entire Page’. This will save the image as a .png file.

If you’re using Chrome, you can also use the same method as Safari. Click the Customise and Control icon and select the ‘Capture Screen’ option. Once you have selected the portion of the page you want to capture, click ‘save’ and the image will be saved as a .png file.
