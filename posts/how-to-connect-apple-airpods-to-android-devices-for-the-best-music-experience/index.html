2. Prerequisites for Connecting AirPods with Android#

Before you can connect your Apple AirPods to an Android device, you must make sure that both devices are compatible. To ensure a smooth connection between the AirPods and the Android device, the device must be running Android 6.0 Marshmallow OS or newer. If your device is running on an earlier OS version, then you won’t be able to use the AirPods. Additionally, you need to make sure that the AirPods are updated to the latest firmware version. You can check the current firmware version by pairing your AirPods with your iPhone or iPad and by launching the ‘Settings’ app.

The next step is to put the AirPods into pairing mode. To do this, you simply need to press and hold the setup button on the back of the AirPods charging case for a few seconds until the LED light flashes Amber. Then, launch the Bluetooth settings on your Android device and select the AirPods from the list of available devices. Once the AirPods are connected, the LED light will flash white.

If you’re using wireless headphone with the AirPods, you need to plug the headphone cable into the audio port of the charging case. Once the AirPods are connected to the headphone, they’ll appear in the Bluetooth list of your Android device. Select the AirPods and you’re good to go.

To enjoy the best sound quality, you should also update the audio driver for the AirPods. To do this, simply launch the Android Device Manager and select the AirPods from the list of available devices. Tap on the ‘Update’ button and wait for the driver to download and install. Your AirPods are now ready to give you the best music experience with your Android device.
