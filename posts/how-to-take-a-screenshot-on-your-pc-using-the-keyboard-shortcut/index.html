It’s worth noting that modern systems such as Windows 10 and macOS have their own built-in screenshot functions—but for the sake of simplicity, let’s focus on the keyboard shortcut method. To begin, you’ll need to make sure that your image editing software of choice is open and active. Windows users should typically use the Paint program, while Mac users should use the Preview application.

Once you’ve got your image editing software open, you can begin to take screenshots using the keyboard shortcut. The most common shortcut is ‘Print Screen’, or ‘PrtScn’ for short. Pressing this button will capture a screenshot of your entire desktop, including all programs, windows and taskbars. To save the image, simply paste the captured image into your image editing program and select ‘Save’.

If you don’t want to include the taskbar and other additional windows in your screenshot, hold down the ‘Alt’ key and press ‘PrtScn’. This will capture only the active window—in other words, the window currently in focus. This is particularly useful if you’d like to capture a specific part of your screen.

Alternatively, you can use the ‘Windows’ key + ‘Shift’ + ‘S’ shortcut to open the Snip & Sketch program in Windows 10. This will allow you to select a specific part of your screen to capture, as opposed to capturing the entire desktop.

In conclusion, taking a screenshot on your PC is incredibly simple using only the keyboard and your favourite image editing software.
