2. Configuring Audio Recording Settings#

Configuring your audio settings correctly is essential when recording audio playing on your Android phone. Setting your recording mode, gain, sample rate, and bit depth correctly will ensure you get a quality recording. Here are some tips to help you set the correct audio recording settings on your Android device.

Firstly, select the recording mode. Most Android phones have an internal microphone as well as an external microphone. To record audio playing on your phone, make sure to select the external microphone on your device’s settings.

Secondly, adjust the gain. You want your gain setting to be as high as possible without recording any distortion. Increasing the gain will increase the volume of the audio recorded, but too much gain will cause distortion.

Thirdly, set the sample rate and bit depth. Sample rate is the number of audio samples taken per second, and bit depth is the number of bits that are used to represent each sample. For most recordings you’ll want to select a sample rate of 44.1 kHz and a bit depth of 16 bits for a good quality recording.

Finally, check your recording settings before you start recording. Make sure all settings are correct and that there is no distortion in the sound. Once you’ve confirmed everything is working correctly, you can start recording audio playing on your Android device.
