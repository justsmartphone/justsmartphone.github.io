Do you want to know how to easily check your Bluetooth device’s battery level on Android? Are you tired of not being able to track your connected Bluetooth device’s battery levels? With that said, here are 5 easy steps to check the battery level on your Bluetooth device in your Android phone.

Step 1: Go to your phone’s settings and select “Connected devices”.

Step 2: Look for the Bluetooth icon and select it.

Step 3: Once the Bluetooth page is located, select the device whose battery level you want to check.

Step 4: You will then be able to view the battery level of the device.

1. Enable Bluetooth Connection on Your Android#

Enabling a Bluetooth connection on your Android device is easy and takes just a few steps. To get started, open the Settings app and head to the Bluetooth options. Depending on your Android device and version, you might have to tap a certain switch, toggle, or button to trigger the Bluetooth connection. Once that is done, your device will automatically search for other Bluetooth-enabled devices. Now, you can check the battery level of the paired Bluetooth device with just a few steps.

First, go to the Settings app and navigate to the Bluetooth menu. From there, select the device which battery level you want to check. Then, tap on the cogwheel icon located next to the device name. This will open the device settings. Here, you will see the battery percentage of the connected device.

To make sure the battery percentage is accurate, tap the options menu in the top right corner of the device settings. From there, select Refresh. This will update the battery level. As an alternative, you can also disconnect and reconnect the device. This will also give you an updated status of the battery level.

In addition to the battery level, you can also check the signal strength of the device. To do so, just select the bar with the ‘RSSI’ icon. From there, you can adjust the signal strength based on your device’s current location.

Finally, make sure to turn off Bluetooth when you’re not using it. This will conserve your device’s battery and also ensures privacy if you’re using a Bluetooth headset. All in all, these are the few easy steps you can follow to check the battery level of any Bluetooth device connected to your Android.
